Week 1 - Design Challenge

Description from user:

My dad loved playing guitar and he suffered a Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) and lost functioning of his right wrist. He still can play all the chords with his left hand but cannot strum the strings with his right hand. I am reaching out to this site to see if you can design a tool that allows my dad to strum a guitar again.

Persona:

Louis Matthews, 51

Background:
Louis went to trade school. He is married and is a father of 2 daughters. He works in construction and had an accident. He suffers from TBI/ heart attack (this can be changed) which made the movement on his right arm and wrist restrictive.

Disability:
It has been 8 months since he had his accident. He can move his arm and forearm slowly but not strong nor fast enough to strum the guitar. His wrist can’t be rotated or moved.

Environment/Scenario:
Before the accident he used to play in a band that performs at the local pub/bar, he wants to continue being a guitarist.

Activity:
The primary function of the device would be to help him strum the guitar. Secondary function would be to help him hold certain items.

Frustrations:
He still has the ability to play chords with his left hand, however he’s unable to move his right arm/hand in order to strum the guitar. The feeling of being able to reach the strings and chords on the guitar, but totally losing the ability to produce sound.

Week 4 - 1:1 Prototype
Material Selection and Fabrication:

Plastic

  • A plastic sleeve will be used to support the arm of the user on the strummer.
  • The sleeve will be 3d printed.

Ceramic/Plaster

  • The frame and shell of the strummer will be moulded with ceramic/plaster.
  • We will mould the frame out of a mould.

Velcro

  • Velcro straps will be attached to the sleeve to secure the arm on it.
  • We will purchase the velcro straps.

Metal Hinge

  • A metal hinge will be installed between the sleeve and the frame of the strummer so that the user can freely rotate the sleeve.
  • We will purchase the metal hinges.
